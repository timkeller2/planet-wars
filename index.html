How to Play

  • Elimination: Be the last player standing. All other players must lose every planet and ship.
  • Tech Victory: Lead the 2nd place player by at least 12 Tech Bonus points (√Tech).
  • Experience Victory: Lead the 2nd place player by at least 12 Experience Bonus points (√Exp).
  • Happiness Victory: Lead the 2nd place player by at least 12 Happiness Bonus points (√Happiness).
  • Score Victory: Have twice the Victory Points of the 2nd place player, with a minimum score of 50 VP (or [game minutes] VP in a timed game).
  • Economic Domination: Control more than 75% of the galaxy's total planet capacity.
  • Timed Game Victory: When the clock runs out, the player with the highest combined Victory Points wins. If no players survive, it's a draw.
  • Player Elimination: Capturing a player's last planet eliminates them, awarding the conqueror +100 Exp. Destroying a Homeworld also awards +100 Exp.
  • Leaderboard Scoring: The leaderboard ranks all players by Victory Points = √Tech + √Exp + √Happiness. Players with a 🎯 bullseye are dominant threats meeting at least 75% of any victory condition (e.g. leads of ≥9 in Tech, Exp, or Happiness, a ≥1.75× VP ratio over 2nd place with at least 75% of the minimum score threshold, or controlling ≥56.25% of galactic capacity) — AIs will gang up on them!
  • Left-click: Select planets or ships. Click empty space to deselect. Click a selected cruiser again to toggle Upgrade Mode.
  • Shift + Left-click: Add/remove planets or ships from your current selection.
  • Shift + Left-drag: Draw a lasso to box-select multiple ships and/or planets.
  • Right-click: Issue orders — attack enemies, reinforce friendlies, or move to empty space. Right-click an enemy ship to target-lock your cruisers or redirect your fleets.
  • Shift + Right-click: Queue orders for selected cruisers. They execute waypoints sequentially.
  • Left-drag / Middle-click Drag: Pan the camera.
  • Scroll Wheel / +/- keys: Zoom in and out (0.2×–5.0×).
  • Arrow Keys: Pan the camera.
  • Tab: Cycle through your owned planets (selects and centers camera).
  • Ctrl+Tab: Cycle through your cruisers.
  • Alt+Tab: Cycle through your active fleets.
  • Ctrl+0–9: Assign selected ships/fleets to a control group.
  • 0–9: Recall a saved control group.
  • Enter: Open/send chat. Type N <name> with a cruiser or planet selected to rename it (max 16 characters).
  • Pause/Break: Pause the game.
  • L: Toggle the leaderboard panel.
  • Touch Controls (Mobile):
    • Quick Tap: Select/deselect a planet or cruiser. Tap a selected unit again to view detailed tooltip. Touch/tap the tooltip to dismiss.
    • Hold 450ms: Issue commands (attack/move). Marked by floating COMMAND ISSUED text.
    • Drag (>10px): Pan the camera.
    • Two-finger Pinch: Zoom in/out smoothly.

Order Modifiers (toggle keybinds):

  • W — Warp: Toggles warp speed/danger for the next fleet launch.
  • B — Bomb Mode / Eco: When a planet is selected, toggles Eco bombers for the next launch. When cruiser(s) are selected, toggles auto-planet-bombardment.
  • Q — Ship Bombers: Toggles ship bombers for the next launch (requires 🚀 Military planet).
  • S — Scout / Scout Mode: When a planet is selected, toggles launching a 10% scouting fleet (requires Tech ≥ 100). When cruiser(s) are selected, toggles autonomous Scout mode.
  • A — Attack: Toggles autonomous Attack/Engage mode for selected cruiser(s).
  • R — Research: Toggles autonomous Research/Lab mode for selected cruiser(s) with Laboratories.
  • D — Dismantle: Dismantles selected cruiser if inside a friendly gravity well (cruisers with the Diplomat upgrade automatically perform diplomacy).
  • P — Patrol: Toggles autonomous Patrol mode for selected cruiser(s). Target-locking a planet automatically deactivates patrol.
  • C — Cruiser Build: Opens the cruiser construction menu (requires 🚀 Military planet or Homeworld).
  • O — Focus Mode: Opens the focus mode selector for a selected owned planet.
  • U — Upgrade Mode: Opens the cruiser upgrade menu (cruiser must be near a friendly planet).
  • Production: Owned planets auto-produce ships. Rate scales with how many ships the planet is missing, boosted by Tech Score. Human production above 1× is reduced by 1/3.
  • Low Population Penalty: Planets below 50 ships suffer reduced production. Below 5 ships, production drops to just 10%.
  • Capacity Growth: Full planets slowly gain +1 max capacity (in Economy focus). Homeworlds gain +2. Research-focused planets earn Tech instead. Garrison-focused planets hold double capacity, don't grow, and grant a starting experience bonus (maxShips/10) to all launched fleets, cruisers, and capital ships built there. Commerce-focused planets generate credits instead.
  • Tech Score 🧪: Earned when planets at max capacity sit in Research focus. Human players have a failure chance based on galaxy capacity percentage (failChance = capacityPercent × 2). Success costs 2 ships and grants +1 Tech (or +2 on 🔬 Research planets). Failure costs 1 ship (🔬 Research planets still gain +1 on failure).
  • Tech Enhancement Bonuses: Each time your √Tech bonus increases by 1, you receive a random -10% upgrade cost discount on one upgrade category (down to -50% max). A floating text and chat message announce the discount.
  • Exp Score 🎯: Earned through combat (every 20 kills = +1 Exp). Eliminating a player or destroying a Homeworld awards +100 Exp. Diplomats also earn Exp through sympathy generation.
  • Credits 💲: A currency earned by Commerce-focused planets. Credits can supplement ship costs when building cruisers, paying for upgrades, and transitioning focus modes — reducing the garrison drain.
  • Resource Stockpile Capacity 📦: Caps the amount of resources you can stockpile before incurring storage fees. Capacity = Command Capacity + Trade Capacity.
  • Gravity Fields: Planets project gravity wells (radius = maxShips × 1.5, expanded by Tech/Exp). Military planets at full capacity get 1.5× gravity radius. Garrison-focused planets at full capacity extend gravity by +ships/2. Friendly wells protect ships from attrition, allow cruiser healing/rearming, and grant starting experience bonuses.
  • Orbital Bombardment: Each defending ship killed has an 8% chance to reduce max capacity by 1. Below 55 max capacity = planet destroyed!
  • Planet Explosion: Destroyed planets deal 3d6 splash damage to all planets within 400px (reduced by 1 per 25px). Max capacity takes 1/3 of that. Military planet explosions deal 3× damage in a 600px radius. Chain reactions are possible!
  • Sacrificial Upgrade: Sending ships to a full friendly planet sacrifices them. Every 20 sacrificed = +1 max capacity.
  • Capture Penalty: Capturing an owned planet costs -1 max capacity. Capturing a neutral has a 30% chance to assign a special type (10% each: Research, Military, Speed).
  • Homeworld 👑: Starting planet. 2× production, +2 capacity growth. Attacking or defending grants +15% combat bonus (human players only). Destroying one awards +100 Exp. Cruisers can be built here.
  • Special Planet Types:
    • 🔬 Research: Earns +2 Tech per successful tick instead of +1. Still earns +1 on failures.
    • 🚀 Military: Required to launch bombers and build cruisers. Full military planets get 1.5× gravity radius. Costs -1 max capacity per bombing launch. Explosions deal 3× damage in 600px radius.
    • ⚡ Speed: Ships launched gain +15 flat speed bonus.
  • Sympathy & Revolts: Planets accumulate sympathy from nearby players (see 🤝 Diplomacy topic). When a non-owner's sympathy exceeds 1/3 of the planet's garrison, a revolt contest can trigger, potentially flipping ownership peacefully!
  • Overview: Every owned planet operates in one of five focus modes. Press O (or click the focus badge) on a selected planet to switch. Focus transitions take 15 seconds and cost half the planet's max capacity in ships (payable with credits). Planets start in a random mode (Economy, Research, or Garrison).
  • 📈 Economy (E): The default growth mode. When the planet is at full capacity, it gains +1 max capacity periodically (+2 for Homeworlds). This is how you expand your economy over time.
  • 🔬 Research (R): When at full capacity, the planet generates Tech Score 🧪 instead of growing capacity. Success rate decreases as your share of galactic capacity increases. Research planets (🔬 type) earn double tech. Essential for Tech Victory!
  • 🛡️ Garrison (G): The planet can hold up to 2× its max capacity in ships, producing beyond the normal cap. It does not grow max capacity or earn tech when full. Garrison planets extend their gravity well radius by +ships/2 when above capacity, and grant a starting experience bonus (maxShips/10) to all launched fleets, cruisers, and capital ships built there. Excess ships are sacrificed for capacity upgrades when transitioning out.
  • 💲 Commerce (C): Requires >100 max capacity to unlock. When at full capacity, the planet generates Credits at a rate proportional to ships above 100. Credits can be used to pay for cruiser construction, upgrades, and focus transitions — reducing the need to drain planet garrisons. Commerce planets do not grow capacity or earn tech.
  • ⛏️ Mining (M): When selected, the planet passively extracts dilithium crystals, merculite, duranium, and other raw sci-fi materials found on it at double rate. If the planet is at full capacity (ships >= max capacity), the extraction rate is doubled again. Stockpiled resources can be traded or sold to the galactic bank.
  • 🌱 Terraforming (T): Available if your tech bonus is higher than 1/5 of the planet's habitability rounded up. While full of ships, it slowly increases habitability score (at 1/3 the rate of Economy growth). If habitability exceeds 6x your tech bonus, the focus automatically reverts to Economy. Special planetary transitions: Jungle-to-Ocean becomes Terran; Desert-to-Tundra becomes Arid; Ocean-to-Arid becomes Terran.
  • Launch Cost: Each launch costs 10 + your planet count − √Tech ships (capped at 250). If you have credits and credits-pay is enabled (default), your credits will automatically be consumed to pay/offset this ship cost on a 1-to-1 basis!
  • Smart Reinforcements: Friendly planet-to-planet reinforcement launches are automated:
    • If the destination is already full, 0 ships are launched, preventing wastage.
    • If the target is not full and the source has enough, exactly the number needed to fill the target is sent.
    • Otherwise, exactly half the source planet's remaining ships are sent.
  • Combat (Planet Attack): 80% base kill chance, minus 1% per 5 defending ships, plus Tech/Exp/Gravity/Swarm modifiers. Minimum kill chance = attacker's Tech+Exp bonuses.
  • Swarm Bonus: +1% kill chance per 10 friendly ships within 100px of the target.
  • Gravity Combat Modifier: +1% kill chance per 50 friendly capacity in gravity wells near the fight. -1% per 50 enemy capacity.
  • Homeworld & Last Stand: +15% combat bonus when attacking or defending a Homeworld (human only). +15% Last Stand bonus defending your last planet. These do not apply to AI vs AI.
  • Human vs Human: Defender gets +2% bonus per surviving AI player. Clear out the AI first!
  • Dogfighting: Ships within 40px (expanded by Tech + Health) fire lasers each second. Base hit chance: (10 + Tech + Exp + ShipExp + maxHealth×5 + bombs×3) / 100.
  • Fleet Attrition: Every 4 seconds, one ship per fleet is destroyed (after a safe window based on Tech+Exp). Ships inside friendly gravity wells take 1/3 attrition.
  • Deep Space Attrition: Ships in deep space take escalating damage the longer they fly, starting after a safe window of (√Tech + √Exp×0.5) seconds. Bombers take half attrition.
  • Distance-Based Launch Speeds: Fleets launched to destinations less than 100px away travel at 1/4 speed (saving rolls enabled). Otherwise normal speed. Cruisers always launch at normal speed.
  • Speed Modifiers: 1/4 speed gets a 90% Saving Roll vs damage. 1/2 speed gets 75%. Cruisers and Amoebas always survive rolls.
  • Warp Drive (W): Adds (30 − your planet count, min 20) speed bonus. Each ship has a (50% − √Tech)% chance to explode on launch!
  • Bombers (B/Q): Military 🚀 planets only. Sends 1/3 of fleet at speed 20. Costs -1 max capacity.
    • B — Eco Bomb: 1d2 damage to planet max capacity.
    • Q — Ship Bomb: 1d4 damage to planet garrison.
  • Hazard Combat Penalty: Nebulae reduce kill chance by intensity/4 %. Ion Storms reduce by intensity/2 % (modified by knowledge/tech).
  • Building Cruisers 🛸: Built from 🚀 Military planets or your Homeworld. Press C to open the build menu. Choose from 5 hull classes (Corvette 15HP to Mammoth 55HP). Cost = ships + max capacity deducted from the planet. Starting XP is capped at the player's overall XP score (`player.expScore`), only consuming as much player `crewExperience` as needed. The planet must retain at least 55 max capacity after construction.
  • Hull Classes:
    • Corvette (S): 15 HP — 50 ships, 2 cap
    • Destroyer (D): 25 HP — 100 ships, 4 cap
    • Battlecruiser (A): 35 HP — 175 ships, 7 cap
    • Titan (T): 45 HP — 300 ships, 12 cap
    • Mammoth (M): 55 HP — 500 ships, 20 cap
  • Movement & Speed: Cruisers always launch at normal speed. Larger cruisers move slower. Engine upgrades add +3 speed per level. Damaged cruisers suffer a speed penalty. Moving at 1/2 or 1/4 speed settings grants hazard saving rolls (75% or 90% save chance).
  • Fuel System: Cruisers consume fuel outside friendly gravity wells. Flying costs 4 fuel/tick (8 in warp). Stationary outside wells costs 2 fuel/tick. Inside friendly wells, fuel regenerates. Base fuel capacity = maxHealth/5 (increased by Engine upgrades and Fuel Tanker).
  • Out of Fuel Penalty: At 0 fuel, cruisers move at 1/4 speed and take 1d6 direct HP damage per tick outside friendly wells.
  • Fuel Transfer: Friendly cruisers within sensor range autonomously share fuel — if one has fuel >1, it donates 1 to a stranded ally.
  • Multi-shot Weapons: Cruisers fire laser shots per second equal to their current health. They target enemy ships, amoebas, and planets.
  • Bomb Sacs: Carry bombs = maxHealth/5 (increased by Munitions). Each bombardment volley on planets uses 1 bomb. Bombs reload inside friendly gravity wells.
  • Armor Deflection: Base shrug chance = (MaxHealth + √Tech + √Exp + √ShipExp)%, capped at 90%. Shields upgrade closes the deflection gap by 20% of the remaining gap to 100% per level. Carrying Duranium resource adds a flat +10% shrug chance. If out of bombs (0), deflection is halved — keep them loaded!
  • Crew System: Cruisers have crew (up to 2× current health). Crew restores at 1/sec inside friendly wells when no enemies are within 100px. Crew is consumed during boarding combat.
  • Patrol Mode (P): Toggled via P key or button. Cruiser attacks enemies in sensor range, flies to a nearby friendly well to rearm, then returns to its patrol station. Target-locking a planet automatically deactivates patrol mode.
  • Bombardment Threat Safety: Cruisers automatically suspend bombardment when any enemy ship or Amoeba is within sensor range, switching to defensive engagement.
  • Order Queueing (Shift+Right-click): Queue movement and targeting orders. Cruisers execute them sequentially, advancing to the next waypoint automatically.
  • Cruiser Bomb Toggle (B): When cruiser(s) are selected, B toggles auto-planet-bombardment on/off for those cruisers.
  • Upgrade Mode (U) ⚙️: Select a cruiser near a friendly planet and press U (or click the cruiser again) to open the upgrade panel. Costs are paid from the nearby planet's garrison (and your credit balance).
  • Cost Formula: Base cost = min(150, 25 + maxHealth × (3 + totalUpgrades/3)), modified by a global discount (-25% base) and personal tech-enhancement discounts (earned as your √Tech increases). Costs scale with the cruiser's total upgrade count.
  • Upgrade Limits: Each category caps at Level 5. Individual upgrade level ≤ maxHealth/10. Total upgrades ≤ maxHealth/5. Bigger hulls support more upgrades.
  • Available Modules:
    • 📡 Sensor Array (S): +25% scan/weapon range per level. Expands the cruiser's detection, attack, and diplomacy radius.
    • 🔬 Laboratories (L): +1 lab per level. Labs accumulate hazard knowledge when near storms/nebulae, reducing their danger. Uses 🔬 icon.
    • 🛡️ Armor Plating (A): +4 flat + 10% maxHealth armor capacity per level. Consuming Duranium resource (if available in player stockpile) boosts this armor capacity bonus by +50%. Absorbs damage before hull.
    • 🌀 Energy Shields (H): Closes the deflection gap by 20% of the remaining gap to 100% per level. Cannot purchase if the next level would exceed the hard cap of 90% total deflection.
    • 🚀 Overcharged Engine (E): +3 speed and +3°/sec turn rate per level. Also adds +2/+1/+1 bonus fuel capacity at Levels 1/2/3.
    • 💣 Munition Pods (M): +1 bomb capacity and +1 splash damage per level. Splash destroys additional fleet ships or deals bonus HP damage to cruisers/amoebas (limited to 1/50th of target's size).
    • 🎯 Targeting Computer (T): +5% weapon range and +5% accuracy per level.
    • 🔧 Damage Control (D): +50% hull repair speed per level inside friendly wells (continues even while in combat). Enables deep-space healing at 20% per level. Mitigates damaged-cruiser speed and volley size reduction by 1/(1+level).
    • ⛽ Fuel Tanker (F): +(maxHealth/5 + 10) fuel capacity, +15 maxsupplies, and +(25 + level*10)% supply cost discount per level. Reduces speed by -3, laser accuracy by -5%, and weapon range by -5 per level.
    • 🤝 Diplomat (I): +1 diplomatic attempt per 60/level seconds. Generates sympathy on neutral and enemy planets in sensor range, potentially flipping them via revolt. Also reduces enemy sympathy on your own planets. Earns Exp on success. See 🤝 Diplomacy topic.
    • 🪖 Marines (R): Marine capacity = level × maxHealth. Automatically loads marines from nearby friendly planets (>50% garrison). Marines launch fleet assaults on enemy planets when they outnumber defenders 3:1. Can also board enemy cruisers when marines exceed 2× enemy crew — capturing the ship! Consumes Tritanium (if available) to grant +400 starting Exp to launched marine fleets.
  • Cancel: Press C, U, Escape, or click ❌ Cancel to exit Upgrade Mode. The cruiser stays selected.
  • Sympathy System: Planets accumulate sympathy toward players, representing soft influence. Sympathy accrues from two sources: nearby owned planets (gravitational influence) and cruiser Diplomat modules.
  • Gravity-Based Sympathy: Owned planets passively exert sympathy on neutral/enemy planets within their gravity well that have fewer ships than them, at a rate of 1 sympathy per minute. Larger, fuller planets naturally extend influence further.
  • Diplomat Sympathy: Cruisers with the 🤝 Diplomat upgrade attempt sympathy rolls on neutral/enemy planets in sensor range every 60/diplomat_level seconds. Success chance = (30 + Disposition + Sympathy + √PlayerExp + √ShipExp)% (or (30 + Disposition + Sympathy + (√PlayerExp + √ShipExp) * 3 + 10)% if you have their preferred resource). Success rolls a d100 vs success chance and increases sympathy by 1 + (chance - roll) / 25 rounded down (doubled if preferred resource is used). Awards +1 player Exp and ship Exp equal to sympathy gained.
  • Disposition: On first successful diplomat contact, a planet rolls a disposition toward you: 10d10 - 60 + (√PlayerExp + √ShipExp) * 3 (if you have > 0.1 of their preferred resource) + 10 (if you have the preferred resource) + 1/2 of any existing sympathy. Higher disposition means larger sympathy gains per success.
  • Sympathy Cap: A player's sympathy on a planet caps at the planet's max capacity. The sum total of all players' sympathy on a planet is also capped at the planet's capacity. Exceeding this total cap wastes 50% of the excess sympathy, and applies the remaining 50% to actively reduce/displace competitors' sympathy on that planet (highest first).
  • Counter-Espionage: If no enemy/neutral planets are in range, diplomats will instead target friendly planets that have enemy sympathy, reducing the highest enemy sympathy by 1 per attempt.
  • Revolts: When a non-owner's sympathy exceeds 1/3 of the planet's garrison, a revolt can trigger. Factions enter a competitive roll with win odds computed via real-time Monte Carlo simulations. The highest roll wins, consuming sympathy equal to 1/3 of the garrison. Successful revolts trigger a 60-second global cooldown (restricting other successful revolts) and grant the planet 180 seconds of immunity. Failed revolts apply a dynamic cooldown to that planet: 60 + (Owner Roll - Highest Challenger Roll) * 3 seconds.
  • Spy Rooted Out 👁️: When your ships are cleared from an enemy planet you attacked, a 👁️ icon appears, indicating your espionage attempt has been discovered.
  • Marine Boarding: Cruisers with Marines can board enemy cruisers when their marine count exceeds 2× the enemy's crew. Boarding sends a pod that initiates combat between marines and the target's crew+marines. Victory captures the enemy cruiser! Surviving marines return to the launching cruiser via return pod.
  • Marine Planet Assaults: When a cruiser's marines exceed 3× a nearby enemy planet's garrison (or the planet is explicitly targeted), it launches the marines as a standard fleet to capture the planet.
  • Ion Storms ⚡: Moving hazards that drift across the map. New storms spawn roughly every 5 minutes. Intensity = 10d6 (capped by spawn count). Ships inside take damage every second, while planets inside take damage every second to their max capacity.
  • Ancient Minefields 💣: Stationary. Intensity = 12d6. Massive burst damage on entry, especially to fast ships. Ongoing damage to ships inside. Do not damage planets.
  • Nebulae 🌫️: Stationary, 30% larger. Intensity = 5d12 (5–60). Slow ships passing through, reduce combat effectiveness by intensity/4 %. No direct damage or entry burst.
  • Sensor Reduction: Hazards reduce your gravity well sensor range while planets/ships are inside them. Effective intensity is displayed as a clean integer on the starmap.
  • Damage Formula:
    • Ion Storms: Hit Chance % = effectiveIntensity = max(0, intensity − knowledge − (√Tech + √Exp)/2 − √ShipExp). Checked every second. Non-moving ships cut their hit chance to 1/4. On hit, cruisers take 1 damage, and standard fleets take 5–15% damage.
    • Minefields: Risk Chance % = (effectiveIntensity × currentSpeed) / 10. Checked every second. Cruisers take 1 damage, and standard fleets take 3d6% damage.
  • Knowledge: Increases when cruisers with 🔬 Laboratories are within scanning range of a hazard (+1 per lab per 2 minutes). Directly reduces effective intensity.
  • Amoeba Immunity: Amoebas are immune to Ion Storm entry damage (but not minefields).
  • Planet Erosion: Ion storms (not minefields) erode planet max capacity over time. Below 55 = destroyed! Last Stand gives +20 knowledge bonus.
  • Visuals: Yellow ⚡ = Ion Storm. Blue 💣 = Minefield. Purple 🌫️ = Nebula.
  • Giant Space Amoebas 🧬: Alien creatures belonging to the "Monsters" faction. They wander the map attacking ships and bombarding planets.
  • Amoeba Hive: The Monsters' homeworld. Spawns a new Amoeba every 2 minutes. Initial Amoebas also spawn scattered across the map at game start (1 per 10 planets).
  • Stats: Speed = 4d6 (varies per Amoeba). Health = 4–7 (random 3d2+1). Attack range = 50px. Rendered at double standard graphic size with organic green plasma spray animations.
  • Regeneration & Armor: Amoebas heal 1 HP per minute. Deflection chance = 50% + 1% per maxHealth + 1% per Tech/Exp modifier (capped at 95%). When shrugging damage, a green ripple animation plays. At 0 health with maxHealth > 0, they lose 1 maxHealth but fully regenerate — shrinking each time.
  • Growth & Splitting: Killing a ship or bombarding a planet grants +1 maxHealth and +1 health, subject to a cooldown of (10 × Amoeba Count × mapScale) seconds. At their splitting threshold (max(4, √Tech)), 50% chance to split into two smaller Amoebas. Splitting feeds the Monster faction's tech and exp scores!
  • Bomb Sacs: Carry bombs equal to maxHealth. Regenerate 1 per 60 seconds without needing a gravity well.
  • Combat Value: Killing an Amoeba awards 10 × maxHealth Exp — valuable targets!
  • Wandering AI: Amoebas roam toward planets, attacking non-Monster ships in range and bombarding unowned/enemy planets.
  • AI Spawning: AIs spawn gradually over the first 4 minutes. Count is configurable (default 5).
  • AI Aggression: Base decision interval = 2.0 seconds, decreasing by 0.2s per planet owned (min 0.5s).
  • Defense Thresholds:
    • Small Empires (<4 planets): Won't attack unless garrison exceeds 75% capacity.
    • Minimum Garrison: Never attacks with ≤12 ships.
    • Threat Reserves: Reserve ratio starts at 70%, drops 5% per planet (min 35%). Only 10% chance to attack if nearby threats (>15 ships within 400px) detected.
  • Launch Worthiness: Source garrison must exceed target by >10 ships.
  • AI Speed Limits: Fleets limited to 1/2 speed until √Tech ≥ 7 (Tech ≥ 49), then normal speed.
  • Overpopulation: AI planets with >160 ships have a 10% chance/10s to auto-attack the weakest enemy.
  • Dominant Player Targeting 🎯: AIs gang up on players exceeding 4,500 capacity, leading tech by 200+, or holding 2× capacity of 2nd place.
  • Homeworld Priority: AIs fiercely retake their lost homeworld (75% chance to immediately attack if in top targets).
  • Rampage Events: Starts at 30 minutes (on normal 1600px maps, scaling shorter on larger maps) and accelerates by decaying 6 minutes each time down to a 12-minute minimum. Selects a neutral planet to undergo a 3-minute incubation phase before awakening:
    • Garrison: +300% of max capacity instantly.
    • Production: 3× rate.
    • Safety: Won't launch until garrison >200% capacity (retains 100%).
    • Decay & Collapse: Planet loses capacity over time and per launch. Below 55 = destroyed! Captured to stop decay.
  • Fog of War: Optional. You can only see within your gravity wells and ship radar. Previously discovered planets appear as silhouettes with cached info.
  • AFK Protection: The game automatically pauses after 5 minutes of global inactivity (no human player actions). Any player command/action will resume the game.
  • Timed Games: Configurable game clock (30 min to unlimited). When time expires, victory is awarded by combined score: √Tech + √Exp + Capacity%.
  • Map Layout: Default 50 planets (scales with map size). Normal sizes are determined by a size class of 60–150 weighted toward the middle. Homeworlds start at size 120 (480 capacity), while Super Planets/Mega-Supers can reach 200–300 capacity.
  • Configurable Settings:
    • AI Count: 0–50 AI players (default 5). Auto-scales with planet count.
    • Fog of War: Toggle visibility restrictions.
    • Small Empires: When enabled, AI players with <4 planets play more defensively.
    • No Rampagers: Disables Rampage events entirely.
    • Background Music: Toggle the soundtrack.
    • Star Field: Toggle the cosmetic background star field.
    • Production Speed: 0.5× (Grand Strategy) to 3× (Blitz) or custom.
    • Hazard Level: 0 (none) to 4× (lots) or custom.
    • Map Size: 800 (Very Small) to 10000 (Galactic) or custom.
    • Planet Count: 10–1000 (default scales with map).
    • Timed Game: 30 min to 3 hours, custom, or unlimited.
  • Leaderboard (L): Ranks all players by Victory Score (√Tech% + √Exp% + Capacity%). Columns: 🧪 Tech, 🎯 Exp, 🪐 Capacity, 🏆 Total. Dominant threats get a 🎯 bullseye.
  • Chat & Naming: Press Enter to chat. Type N <name> to rename a selected cruiser or owned planet (max 16 characters). Chat commands battle time! and meg! play special music tracks.
  • Player Name: Your name is saved locally and remembered between sessions.
  • Mobile: Fully responsive for portrait/landscape. Action buttons stack into compact rows. Leaderboard and menus are scrollable.

Planetary anomalies have a chance of appearing the first time a player discovers a neutral planet. Cruisers with Laboratories can research them when in sensor range.

  • Spawn Probability: 30% + 10% * maxLabs of the discovering ship. If another player has already discovered the planet, this chance is reduced by 25%.
  • Research Process: Cruisers with labs progress research at an experience-boosted rate. Each completed research cycle has a chance: (local ship xp bonus * 3 + player xp bonus - player tech bonus)% to reward the player with 🧪 tech points.
  • Cruiser XP: Completing a research cycle awards +1 local XP to the cruiser.
  • Instant Resolution: Anomalies with a difficulty of 0 or negative can be resolved instantly by any cruiser in range.
  • Completion Rewards: Fully researching an anomaly yields one of 6 random rewards:
    • Upgrade Discounts: 1 + floor(difficulty / 20) discounts of 15% on cruiser upgrade modules.
    • Credits: (30 + difficulty) * (100 - 200)% credits.
    • Tech Score: 1/10 of equivalent credit value added directly to tech score.
    • Player XP: 1/10 of equivalent credit value added to crew experience score.
    • Planet Habitability: Increases planet habitability by 1/5 of equivalent credit value.
    • Upgrade Tokens: 1 + floor(credit value / 40) upgrade tokens rewarded directly to the cruiser.

Anomaly Classification & Styles:

  • 🟢 Faint Spark (Difficulty ≤ 10): Faint green star. Very simple signature, easily scanned or instantly resolved.
  • 🟡 Glowing Core (Difficulty 11 to 35): Yellow star with a central core. Standard energy pattern.
  • 🔵 Pulsing Nova (Difficulty 36 to 60): Rotating cyan star. Medium complexity anomaly.
  • 🟠 Radiant Star (Difficulty 61 to 85): Orange star with a pulsating outer ring. High complexity.
  • 🔴 Quantum Rift (Difficulty 86 to 100): Magenta star with jittering crosshairs and sparks. Highly complex quantum anomaly.
