How to Play

  • Left-click: Select planets or ships. Click empty space to deselect.
  • Left-click + Drag: Draw a lasso to box-select ships or planets. Hold Shift to add/remove from selection.
  • Right-click: Issue orders! Right click an enemy to attack, a friendly planet to reinforce, or empty space to move ships there.
  • Right-click Drag / Middle-click Drag: Pan the camera.
  • Scroll Wheel / +/- keys: Zoom in and out.
  • Arrow Keys: Pan the camera.
  • Touch Controls (Mobile):
    • Selection: Quick tap on a planet or cruiser to select/deselect it.
    • Issue Commands: Tap and **hold for 450ms** on an enemy planet or empty space to attack or move (marked by a floating COMMAND ISSUED neon text). This prevents accidental selections from clearing when ordering attacks!
    • View Stats (Tooltips): Touch down or slowly slide your finger over a planet, cruiser, or space amoeba to display its detailed status overlay.
    • Camera Pan: Drag your finger quickly (>10px) to slide the camera (automatically hides tooltips to keep the view clean).
    • Pinch to Zoom: Two-finger pinch to zoom in/out smoothly.
  • Enter: Open/send chat messages.
  • Pause/Break: Pause the game.
  • Speed Keys (,, ., /): Set fleet speed to 1/4, 1/2, or normal. Keys . and / also set the default speed for future launches.
  • W — Warp: Toggle warp mode for the next launch.
  • B — Bomb Eco: Launch eco bombers (requires 🚀 Military planet).
  • Q — Bomb Ships: Launch ship bombers (requires 🚀 Military planet).
  • F — Fill Mode: Toggle Fill Mode. Sends only enough ships to fill the target friendly planet. (Shortcut only; HUD button is eliminated).
  • S — Scout Mode: Toggle Scout Mode. Sends a small scouting party of 10% of garrison (min 3 ships). (Shortcut only; HUD button is eliminated).
  • C — Cruiser: Toggle Cruiser construction mode from a 🚀 Military planet or your Homeworld.
  • U — Upgrade Mode / Click Toggle: Enter Upgrade Mode when a cruiser is selected inside a friendly gravity well. You can also simply click the currently selected cruiser again to enter Upgrade Mode! Click it again in Upgrade Mode to exit. Cruiser and selection are preserved on exit.
  • Focus Mode Click Toggle: Enter Focus Mode by clicking a selected planet's focus badge (🔬). Click the badge again to exit. Planet and selection are preserved on exit.
  • Upgrade Shortcuts (S, L, A, H, E, M, T, D, C): During active Upgrade Mode, S/L/A/H/E/M/T/D purchase upgrades, and C cancels. Standard commands are temporarily bypassed.
  • L — Leaderboard: Toggle the leaderboard panel.
  • Help Button ❓: Open this How-to-Play help index (integrated into the bottom-right action buttons panel for instant touch accessibility).
  • Elimination: Be the last player standing. All other players must lose every planet and ship.
  • Tech Domination: Achieve a √Tech bonus of at least 15% AND lead 2nd place by at least 10 percentage points.
  • Economic Domination: Control more than 75% of the galaxy's total planet capacity.
  • Player Elimination: Capturing a player's last planet eliminates them, awarding the conqueror +100 Exp.
  • Production: Owned planets auto-produce ships. Rate scales with how many ships the planet is missing, boosted by Tech Score. Human production above 1× is reduced by 1/3.
  • Low Population Penalty: Planets below 50 ships suffer reduced production. Below 5 ships, production drops to just 10%.
  • Capacity Growth: Full planets slowly gain +1 max capacity. Homeworlds gain +2.
  • Tech Score 🧪 & Failures: Earned when planets sit at max capacity. Human players have a failure chance based on galaxy capacity percentage (failChance = capacityPercent * 2). Successful research ticks cost 2 ships; failed ticks cost 1 ship (with no tech score progress, unless on a Research Planet which grants +1 Tech Score anyway).
  • Research Focus 🔬: Transitioning your planet's focus to Research uses the microscope emoji 🔬 for all UI badges, transition bars, and active indicator displays. Beaker 🧪 represents standard planetary natural Research and galactic Tech Score.
  • Exp Score 🎯: Earned through combat (every 20 kills = +1 Exp). Eliminating a player or destroying a Homeworld awards +100 Exp. √Exp × 0.5 provides combat bonuses.
  • Gravity Fields: Planets project gravity wells (radius = maxShips × 1.5, expanded by Tech/Exp). Friendly wells protect ships from attrition and allow cruiser healing/rearming. Gravity gives +1% kill bonus per 50 friendly capacity nearby, -1% per 50 enemy.
  • Orbital Bombardment: Each defending ship killed has an 8% chance to reduce max capacity by 1. Below 55 max capacity = planet destroyed!
  • Planet Explosion: Destroyed planets deal 3d6 splash damage to all planets within 400px (reduced by 1 per 25px). Max capacity takes 1/3 of that. Military planet explosions deal 3× damage in a 600px radius. Chain reactions are possible!
  • Sacrificial Upgrade: Sending ships to a full friendly planet sacrifices them. Every 20 sacrificed = +1 max capacity.
  • Capture Penalty: Capturing an owned planet costs -1 max capacity. Capturing a neutral has a 40% chance to assign a special type.
  • Homeworld 👑: Starting planet. 2× production, +2 capacity growth. Attacking or defending grants +15% combat bonus (human players only). Destroying one awards +100 Exp.
  • Special Planet Types (30% chance on neutral capture):
    • 🔬 Research (10%): Earns +2 Tech per tick instead of +1, marked by the microscope emoji 🔬.
    • 🚀 Military (10%): Required to launch bomber runs and cruisers. Costs -1 max capacity per bombing launch.
    • ⚡ Speed (10%): Ships launched gain +15 flat speed bonus.
  • Launch Cost: Each launch costs 10 + your planet count − √Tech ships. Invest in Tech to launch cheaply!
  • Smart Reinforcements: Friendly planet-to-planet reinforcement launches are automated:
    • If the destination is already full, 0 ships are launched, preventing wastage.
    • If the target is not full and the source has enough garrison, exactly the number of ships needed to fill the target are sent.
    • Otherwise, exactly half the source planet's remaining ships are sent.
  • Combat (Planet Attack): 80% base kill chance, minus 1% per 5 defending ships, plus Tech/Exp/Gravity/Swarm modifiers. Minimum kill chance = attacker's Tech+Exp bonuses.
  • Swarm Bonus: +1% kill chance per 10 friendly ships within 100px of the target.
  • Homeworld & Last Stand: +15% combat bonus when attacking or defending a Homeworld (human only). +15% Last Stand bonus defending your last planet. These do not apply to AI vs AI.
  • Human vs Human: Defender gets +2% bonus per surviving AI player. Clear out the AI first!
  • Dogfighting: Ships within 40px (expanded by Tech + Health) fire lasers each second. Base hit chance: (10 + Tech + Exp + ShipExp + maxHealth×5 + bombs×3) / 100.
  • Fleet Attrition: Every 4 seconds, one ship per fleet is destroyed (after a safe window based on Tech+Exp). Ships inside friendly gravity wells take 1/3 attrition.
  • Deep Space Attrition: Individual ships in deep space take escalating damage the longer they fly, starting after a safe window of (√Tech + √Exp×0.5) seconds. Bombers take half attrition.
  • Distance-Based Launch Speeds: Fleets launched into space travel at **1/4 speed** (saving rolls enabled) if their destination is less than **100px** away from the origin planet. Otherwise, they launch at **normal speed**. Note: Cruisers always launch at normal speed.
  • Speed Modifiers (1/2/3): 1/4 speed gets a 90% Saving Roll vs damage. 1/2 speed gets 75%. Cruisers and Amoebas always survive rolls.
  • Warp Drive (W): Adds (30 − your planet count, min 5) speed bonus. Each ship has a (50% − √Tech)% chance to explode on launch!
  • Bombers (B/Q): Military 🚀 planets only. Sends 1/3 of fleet at speed 20. Costs -1 max capacity.
    • B — Eco Bomb: 1d2 damage to planet max capacity.
    • Q — Ship Bomb: 1d4 damage to planet garrison.
  • Hazard Combat Penalty: Nebulae reduce kill chance by intensity/4 %. Ion Storms reduce by intensity/2 % (modified by knowledge/tech).
  • Cruisers 🛸: Heavy warships equipped with massive health pools and regenerating weapon systems (bombs).
  • Cruiser Stats & Launches: Health = garrison/25. Cost = health×15 ships + health×2 max capacity. Speed decreases with health. Cruisers **always launch at normal speed**, unaffected by launch distances. They orbit enemy planets instead of landing.
  • Upgrade Accessibility: You can enter Upgrade Mode on owned planets regardless of their ship garrison size. Insufficient garrison ships will gray out and disable unaffordable options while keeping costs visible.
  • Fuel System & Attrition: Cruisers consume fuel outside friendly gravity wells. Flying drains fuel at **twice the rate** (4 in sub-warp flight, 8 in warp). Inside friendly wells, they naturally recover fuel.
  • Out of Fuel Penalty: When out of fuel (fuel <= 0), cruisers move at **1/4 speed** and take **1d6 direct health damage** (bypassing armor) each time fuel is consumed outside a friendly gravity well.
  • Autonomous Fuel Transfer: Out-of-fuel friendly cruisers within sensor range of each other will autonomously share fuel. If one has fuel > 1, it transfers **1 fuel** to rescue the stranded cruiser.
  • Multi-shot: Cruisers fire multiple laser shots per second equal to their current health, targeting enemy ships and planets.
  • Bomb Sacs: Cruisers carry bombs equal to 1/5 of their max health. Each dogfight volley at planets consumes one bomb sac. Bombs reload at friendly gravity wells (base rate of 1 per 10 seconds, but reload rate increases by +10% per max bomb capability).
  • Armor Deflection (Damage Resistance): Cruisers have a base chance to completely shrug off incoming damage equal to (Max Health + √Tech + √Exp × 0.5 + √ShipExp × 0.5)% (capped at 75%). The **Shields** upgrade directly adds +10% (Tier 1), +15% (Tier 2), or +20% (Tier 3) to this shrug chance before capping. If bomb sacs are empty (0), this deflection chance is **cut in half**. Keep them armed!
  • Splash, Targeting, & Damage Control: Cruisers can be upgraded with **Munitions** to gain splash damage (destroying multiple standard fleet ships or dealing extra direct HP damage to cruisers/amoebas limited to 1/25th target size when bombs are loaded, i.e. <25 no splash, <50 1 splash, <75 2 splash, <100 3 splash), **Targeting Computers** (boosts accuracy and range by up to +20%), and **Damage Control** (accelerates repairing by up to +3 HP/min and reduces damage speed penalties by up to 75%).
  • Bombardment Threat Safety: Cruisers will automatically suspend planetary bombardment if there is any active enemy ship or wild Space Amoeba within their sensor range, switching their weapon systems entirely to defensive engagement.
  • Cruiser Upgrade Mode (U) ⚙️: Select a Cruiser and click it again (or press U) to enter Upgrade Mode. Upgrade choices are grayed out/disabled if the planet does not have enough ships.
  • Deduction Cost: Every upgrade costs exactly 100 ships deducted directly from the nearby friendly planet.
  • Max Upgrades: Each category can be upgraded a maximum of 3 times. When a category is fully upgraded, its button disappears from the Upgrade Mode overlay.
  • Progression & Diminishing Returns: Tier 2 and Tier 3 upgrades do not decay consecutively. Instead, both Tier 2 and Tier 3 grant the exact same bonus—half of the first upgrade's effect (minimum increment of 1 / 1% / 1.0).
  • Upgrade Emojis: Active upgrade emojis are drawn at **half size** (4px) on the starmap under the cruisers for a clean layout.
  • Available Modules:
    • 📡 Sensor Array (S): Increases sensor scanning range (+50% Tier 1, +25% Tier 2, +25% Tier 3, up to +100% total range boost).
    • 🔬 Laboratories (L): Increases research lab speed (+1 Lab each tier, up to +3 total), allowing the cruiser to accumulate hazard knowledge. Uses the microscope emoji 🔬.
    • 🛡️ Armor Plating (A): Increases maximum and current hull health (+6 HP Tier 1, +3 HP Tier 2, +3 HP Tier 3, up to +12 total HP).
    • 🌀 Energy Shields (H): Boosts cruiser shrug/deflection rates (+10% deflection Tier 1, +5% Tier 2, +5% Tier 3, up to +20% total).
    • 🚀 Overcharged Engine (E): Enhances cruising speed (+10 speed Tier 1, +5 speed Tier 2, +5 speed Tier 3, up to +20 total speed) AND increases maximum fuel capacity and fuel levels (+2 fuel Tier 1, +1 fuel Tier 2, +1 fuel Tier 3).
    • 💣 Munition Pods (M): Boosts ammunition/bomb capacity (+2 max bombs Tier 1, +1 Tier 2, +1 Tier 3) AND increases passive splash damage (+1 splash rating per tier). When the cruiser has bombs (bombs > 0) and splash damage is active, hitting targets automatically destroys additional standard fleet ships or deals extra direct damage to enemy cruisers/amoebas. Note: splash damage cannot exceed 1/25 of the target's size (remaining fleet count or HP, i.e. <25 no splash, <50 1 splash, <75 2 splash, <100 3 splash).
    • 🎯 Targeting Computer (T): Increases weapon accuracy (+10% hit rate Tier 1, +5% Tier 2, +5% Tier 3) AND expands weapon range by the same percentage.
    • 🔧 Damage Control (D): Restores more hull integrity when healing in a friendly gravity well (+1/+1/+1 HP/min) and decreases the speed penalty for damage to 1/2 (Tier 1), 1/3 (Tier 2), or 1/4 (Tier 3) of its normal impact.
  • Cancel Upgrade Mode: Click the selected cruiser again (or press C or click ❌ Cancel) to exit Upgrade Mode. The cruiser remains selected.
  • Ion Storms ⚡: Moving hazards that drift across the map. New storms spawn roughly every 5 minutes. Intensity = 10d6 (capped by spawn count). Ships and planets inside take periodic damage.
  • Ancient Minefields 💣: Stationary. Intensity = 12d6. Massive burst damage on entry, especially to fast ships. Ongoing damage to ships inside. Do not damage planets.
  • Nebulae 🌫️: Stationary, 30% larger. Intensity = 10d8. Slow ships passing through, reduce combat effectiveness by intensity/4 %. No direct damage or entry burst.
  • Integer Display: Effective intensity (intensity minus knowledge/tech/exp reductions) is rounded and displayed as a clean integer on the starmap.
  • Damage Formula: Chance = (intensity − knowledge − (√Tech + √Exp)/2 − √ShipExp) / divisor. Higher stats = much safer.
  • Knowledge: Increases when cruisers with laboratories (🔬) are within scanning range of a hazard (+1 per lab per 2 minutes). Directly reduces effective intensity.
  • Speed & Entry: Ships traveling faster than base speed (warp) take 3× entry damage from storms/minefields. Slow ships get Saving Rolls.
  • Amoeba Immunity: Amoebas are immune to Ion Storm entry damage (but not minefields).
  • Planet Erosion: Ion storms (not minefields) erode planet max capacity over time. Below 55 = destroyed! Last Stand gives +20 knowledge bonus.
  • Visuals: Yellow ⚡ = Ion Storm. Blue 💣 = Minefield. Purple 🌫️ = Nebula.
  • Giant Space Amoebas 🧬: Alien creatures belonging to the "Monsters" faction. They wander the map attacking ships and bombarding planets.
  • Amoeba Hive: The Monsters' homeworld. Spawns a new Amoeba every 2 minutes. Initial Amoebas also spawn scattered across the map at game start (1 per 10 planets).
  • Stats: Speed = 4d6 (varies per Amoeba). Health = 1–3 (random). Attack range = 50px. Amoebas render at double the standard graphic size and attack targets or planets with a beautiful, custom-designed organic green plasma particle spray animation.
  • Regeneration & Armor: Amoebas slowly heal over time (1 HP per minute). They possess incredibly thick shields/hides, giving them a 50% + 3% per Max Health + 2% per Tech/Exp modifier chance to completely deflect hits (capped at 90%). When shrugging damage, a mini green shrug-ripple animation triggers at a randomized coordinate within the visual boundary of the Amoeba. When reduced to 0 health, if maxHealth > 0, they lose 1 maxHealth but fully regenerate — shrinking visually each time.
  • Growth & Splitting: Killing an enemy ship or bombarding a planet grants +1 maxHealth and +1 health (30-second cooldown). If its maxHealth reaches the splitting threshold (Math.max(4, techBonus)), it has a 50% chance to split into two smaller Amoebas, each taking half of the parent's maxHealth and health. Splitting also feeds the Monster faction, adding experience and tech score equal to the parent's pre-split maxHealth!
  • Bomb Sacs: Amoebas carry bomb sacs equal to their maxHealth. These regenerate naturally (1 per 60 seconds) without needing a friendly well.
  • Combat Value: Killing an Amoeba awards 10 × maxHealth Exp to the attacker — they're valuable targets!
  • Wandering AI: Amoebas roam randomly toward planets. They attack any non-Monster ships in range and bombard unowned or enemy planets.
  • AI Spawning: AIs spawn gradually over the first 4 minutes of the game (interval scales dynamically based on the active AI count). The count is configurable at game start (default 5).
  • AI Aggression: AIs act and make decisions more frequently as they grow. The base decision interval is 2.0 seconds, which decreases by 0.2 seconds per planet owned, down to a hyper-aggressive minimum of 0.5 seconds.
  • Defense Thresholds & Safety Reserves:
    • Small Empires: AI players with fewer than 4 planets will not launch attacks from any planet unless its garrison exceeds 75% capacity.
    • Minimum Garrison: AI planets will never launch attacks if they have 12 or fewer ships.
    • Threat Safety Reserves: The AI calculates a launch reserve ratio (starts at 70% and decreases by 5% per planet owned, down to a 35% minimum). If a launch would drop the garrison below this reserve ratio, the AI scans for hostile planets within 400px with >15 ships. If any significant threats are detected, there is only a 10% chance the AI will risk launching.
  • Launch Worthiness: AIs will only launch an attack if the source planet's garrison exceeds the target planet's garrison by more than 10 ships.
  • AI Speed Limits: If the AI player's √Tech bonus is < 7 (Tech Score < 49), its launched fleets are limited to 1/2 Speed. Once they reach 49+ Tech, fleets launch at Normal Speed (1.0).
  • Overpopulation: AI planets with >160 ships have a 10% chance every 10 seconds to automatically launch an attack at the weakest enemy planet.
  • Dominant Player Targeting 🎯: AIs coordinate to prioritize and target dominant leaders! A player is marked as a dominant threat if they exceed 4,500 total capacity, hold a Tech Score lead of 200+ points, or possess at least double the capacity of the second-place player. Targets owned by a dominant threat receive a huge priority boost (subtracting 200 from the target score).
  • Homeworld Priority: AIs will fiercely defend and retake their homeworld if captured. Retaking their lost homeworld is given an extremely high priority (subtracting 500 from target score), and if it is within their top targets, they have a 75% chance to attack it immediately.
  • Rampage Events: Triggered roughly every 15 minutes (scaled by map size, and accelerating by −3 minutes per event down to a 6-minute minimum). A dormant AI player awakens on a neutral planet, targeting the smallest neutral planet with >150 capacity (or the largest neutral overall).
    • Rampage Garrison: The planet is instantly reinforced with +300% of its max capacity in ships.
    • Vicious Production: Ships are produced at a 3× rate.
    • Garrison Safety: The AI will not launch from a Rampage planet of >= 150 capacity unless its garrison exceeds 200% of max capacity, and it must retain at least 100% capacity after launching.
    • Capacity Decay & Collapse: Rather than growing when full, the planet **loses 1 max capacity** periodically. Additionally, **every ship launch permanently drains 1 max capacity**. If its maximum capacity drops below 55, the planet undergoes catastrophic collapse and is **completely destroyed**! This decay persists until the planet is successfully captured by another player.
  • Fog of War: Optional. You can only see within your gravity wells and ship radar. Previously discovered planets appear as silhouettes.
  • AFK Protection: After 3 minutes of inactivity, your empire is handed to an AI.
  • Map Layout: Default 60 planets. Sizes: Mega-Super (300 cap), Super (250), Large (150–180), Medium (120–150), Small (75–115), Tiny (53–74). Huge maps get more super planets.
  • Configurable Settings: AI count, fog of war, production speed (0.5×–3×), hazard level (0–4×), map size (800–3200), and planet count — all set from the start screen.
  • Leaderboard: Shows players ranked by total capacity. 🧪 = √Tech bonus %. 🎯 = √Exp×0.5 bonus %. 🪐 = capacity (% of galaxy).
  • Mobile responsive Viewport: Fully optimized for portrait/upright iPhone layouts. Action buttons (including the Help ❓ button) stack neatly into two compact horizontal rows with optimized touch target sizes, the leaderboard scales to the top-right with scroll limits, and the options/menus have scrollable areas to avoid cutoffs.
  • Player Name: Your name is saved locally and remembered between sessions.
